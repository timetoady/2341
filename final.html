Artist: Hamasaki Ayumi

Song: Evolution
Album: I am...

During the height of her career, Hamasaki was dubbed the "Empress of J-pop" because of her popularity in Japan and Asia. A noted stylist and lyricist, she has sold over 80 million records, making her the best-selling Japanese solo artist of all time.

One of Ayumi's specialties are deep dives into the Electronica/Dance genre. "Evolution" has a fast pace that takes the occasional breath, than gets even faster, building to a blistering intrumental in the latter half before she works into a high-velocity finish. This song and its remixes were regulars for "Dance Dance Revolution" in arcades all over Japan for a reason.

Artist: Bless4

Song: EXTRA MAGIC HOUR
Album: EXTRA MAGIC HOUR

Bless4 is a four-sibling group based out of Kawasaki, Japan, but most of it's members were born in Provo, UT while their father attended school, moving back to Japan years later. Accordingly, all members are true bi-langual. The lead vocalalist, Akino, also has a solo career, having sung opening songs to several different animes, starting with "Genesis of Aquarion" in 2005.

EXTRA MAGIC HOUR was the opening to the anime adaptation of the light novel series by Shoji Gatoh (of "Full Metal Panic!" fame), "Amagi Brilliant Park." It's cheery, whimsical, celebratory tone stands out as its defining feature, and takes advantage of Akino's ability to put vocal excitement in her songs. She excells at powerful, crisp, straightforward melodies, and emphatic expressions, and EXTRA MAGIC HOUR accentuates this. The song also has an English-language International Edition.

Artist: M.O.V.E.

Song: DOGFIGHT
Album: Initial D Final Best Collection

With their being attached to the Initial D anime franchice, the two-person group of M.O.V.E. became the soundtrack of Japanese racing culture. Their songs typically employ powerful, driving rythms and a balance between rap and vocals handed back and forth.

This song, DOGFIGHT, came at the height of their popularily during the fourth season of the show. It employs some of their best work. Be careful listening to it while driving.
